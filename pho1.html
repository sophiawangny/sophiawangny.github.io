Overview

Pho#1 is a local Vietnamese and Asian fusion restaurant located in Waltham. Pho#1 offers dine-in, delivery, and pickup options. Located in a college town and being right outside Boston, Pho#1 attracts college students, people living in the Waltham/Boston community, and people visiting from outside areas.

Why Redesign? Why is it Important?

I was hungry one night, and decided to scroll through
and look at different restaurant near me. When I clicked
on their websites, I noticed that all of them were outdated
and difficult to traverse.
Especially due to the pandemic, local businesses have
been struggling to stay afloat, and having a good website
can be important outlets for businesses to stand out and attract hungry customers.

    The Research

    Survey

    Main Findings:

  • 60% of surveyed people stated they visited restaurant websites frequently
  • 47% of surveyors said they would never or rarely visit a restaurant's website again after having already ordered/eaten from them before
  • 76% of surveyors said they look at a restaurant website through the phone
  • Top categories surveyors looked at on a restaurant site was prices, menu, and photos of food on a restaurant. The others include restaurant hours, reservations, and links to delivery
  • Top categories surveyors looked at when clicking on a restaurant site they have visited before include photos of food, menu, restaurant's number, and links to delivery

    • Info/Needs/Conclusions Gathered:

  • People do use restaurant websites and check it out often
  • Site should be clear and eye-catching, as it could very likely be the first time users are encountering and interacting with the restaurant brand and info
  • Site should be very mobile-friendly and responsive for users
  • Photos of food, menu, restaurant's number, and links to delivery should not be moved so far away from original design, because it is what regular customers who are familiar with the site are used to.



    • Content Audit

    I created a content audit evaluating all pages and features on the Pho#1 website.This allowed me to organize my work and use experience design principles to comment on what aspects worked and could stay and what could be changed in the redesign.

    Info/Concerns Gathered:

  • Multiple buttons were hidden as text, had incorrect affordances
  • Menu was very long, hard to scroll through and read
  • Navigation bar was not in fixed area, making it harder for users traverse pages
  • Site had many inconsistent titles, buttons, categories

    • Affinity Mapping

    Main Findings:

  • 3rd party sites like Yelp and TripAdvisor are visited mostly for credibility and unbiased reviews
  • People visit restaurant sites generally for easy and quick access to direct, detailed, and updated information about the restaurant
  • When visiting restaurant sites for the first time, people are looking for what is special about the restaurant and to familiarize themselves with food options offered

    • Info/Needs/Conclusions Gathered:

  • A restaurant website's purpose should not to be to replace, or even be better than 3rd party websites.
  • Users trust third party sites like Yelp and Google Reviews to give unbiased, honest reviews, and a restaurant website will never have that credibility or play that kind of role
  • Site should be convenient, easy to use, and efficient to access.
  • It is important to show new people to Pho1 what the restaurant offers and what makes them so special
  • Site should have menu that is clear for both the people who are unfamiliar with Pho1 and eat at Pho1 regularly

    Shortcomings and Next Steps

  • Could've surveyed more evenly. Although we had a big age range of people fill out our survey, about 70% of surveyors were 18-24 years old.
  • This redesign could've been more specific for Pho#1 and Pho#1 customers
  • I have not gotten the chance to test the product, which is something important in the UX Design process
  • Design is an iterative process, and this design is not finished yet. I would love to keep testing, prototyping, researching

    • I would like to...
  • Conduct a even more user research to other age ranges, have more surveyors, conduct interviews to go even deeper
  • Create a Working Prototype, and do testing on the project
  • Show Pho#1 my findings, and talk to them what they would want their customers to know about their restaurant
  • Code up the website and create a clear template that local restaurants can use if they cannot afford a web designer :D
