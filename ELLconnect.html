Overview

As the coordinator for free local ELL(English Language Learning) classes in my college town, Waltham, every semester, when registering people for classes, I receive countless emails from people saying that they cannot attend class, for a variety of reasons, including distance, class format, time, and more.
This led to me ask a whole variety of questions: How do people in the area look for places that offer English classes? How many different methods do people have to try before finding the right one? How has looking for English classes changed since the pandemic? Is there a platform that lists English classes in the area? As you can see, the answer was no.

Problem

Many adult immigrants in the Waltham area struggle to find free/low-cost English classes that fit their schedule, constraints, and needs

Approach

A central platform that allows users to search and sign up for a free/low-cost English class in the Waltham area
Research and ideation gathered through:
  • Survey
  • Interviews with different stakeholders
  • Persona

    • Goals

  • Demystify the experience - An engaging, non-intimidating, personal experience
  • Intuitive and easy-to-use product

    • Process

    Research -> Ideate -> Design -> Test -> Iterate




    User Research

    ELL Class Providers/Orgs.

    Interview with ELL Director of the Waltham Public Library
  • Current practice for distributing ELL resource is through posting + emailing flyers and manually updating them as the classes fill up or registration closes
  • Outreach and promotion of English classes from one class provider to the other is usually done through intentional connections that is built over time with different organizations
  • Information about ELL classes are distributed through email, walk-in, calling, and online


    • Interview with Director of Community Service
  • There could be potential language and technology problems, as well as problems with understanding of a webpage to the target population of users

    • ELL learners in Waltham Area

    Survey
  • A major pain point that our respondents experience is not finding courses offered at times that they are available
  • The library is used often to look for English classes, but that is not their preference. Instead, people prefer looking online, searching on a computer or using emails for English classes
  • Despite already being in an English class, surveyors are often searching for English classes in Waltham actively
  • Some learners feel more comfortable searching for English classes in their native language


    • Interviews
  • Some learners are not the most comfortable with/used to creating an account on a website
  • The distance of a class is a factor
  • The teachers are a big part of what makes an English class: Learners go for the English skills, but stay for the teachers.


    • Insights

  • Platform should not replace the use of emails or flyers-- instead, it should work with the current practices on top of being an additional tool
  • Communications between English class providers in Waltham are not unified, leading to difficulty in search and lack of centralization of ELL resources
  • Accessing website should not create barriers including requirement of creation of accouunt or just a non-translateable site
  • Platform should be able to guide a person to find a class that caters to their specific wants/needs

    Key Features

  • Homepage with free English classes without requiring sign in
  • Optional filters including preferred dates and times, distance, class format, etc.
  • "Get Printable PDF" button to print paper/flyer alternative
  • "About Your Teacher" section to get to know the teacher beforehand
  • Language switching option
  • An online traditonal calendar with color coded visualization of class time blocks
  • Guiding instructions for registrations, signing in, signing up, and calendar help

    • Reflection & Future Considerations

    Intentionally or not, technology has been used as a tool to create barriers for underrepresented communities — how can I use tech to benefit these communities, and not block them out like technology has in the past and continues to do? How can I research, design, and create with intent and everyone in mind? While I ask these questions while I create and design with that in mind, I cannot help but feel helpless to the fact that people who might benefit from the use of a site like this might not have access to view it.
