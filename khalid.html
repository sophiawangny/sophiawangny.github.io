K H A L I D   R E B R A N D

Graphic Designer

What:

   Creative Project

When:

   Feb-May 2021

Tools:

   Adobe Creative Suite
In the Spring semester of 2021, I cross-enrolled in Boston University's Design&New Media Class, MET CM 744. Tasked to create rebranding for the musical artist of our choice, I took on 23 year-old musician, Khalid, who became popular at the young age of 17, writing about life as a teen in El Paso, Texas. Using Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, Indesign, I created a unique, fresh, and meaningful rebranding for Khalid.

Target Audience

American teenagers, ~17-22 year old

Demographic Lifestyle

  • The everyday teen with everyday problems with love, family, and finding purpose in life
  • They have free time to hang out, think about life, commute to school, etc.

    • Music Genres

  • R & B
  • Pop
  • Rock
  • Hip Hop
  • Soul

    • Goal

    Overall, the goal of this rebranding is to allow Khalid fans and listeners to feel nostalgia, relaxation, and refresh from these ads, posters and logos. Khalid's music is something that people can relax and bop to for hours, and it is also music that makes people think about the past and how life as a teen was/is. Having a brand experience fit the music experience is important and crucial in making the artist as a whole stand out and be recognized clearly. Khalid's music is relatable because they are songs that talk about being young, naive, and in love, which many teenagers can listen to and understand, and my aim was to achieve that feeling through this rebrand.

    Color Palette

    After examining Khalid's music, past albums, style, and audience, it was quickly noticed that Khalid's brand includes lots of blue and orangish-yellows.
  • Khalid's identity is represented in colors -- His past albums seem to mostly take place in settings of of blue skies and orange deserts, which is the setting of El Paso, the place where he considers filled with memories and nostalgia
  • Khalid has a mixture of slow and upbeat pieces- The blue is cool and relaxing, but can also represent the slow songs he writes/sings, the orange represents the more upbeat, firey songs he has
