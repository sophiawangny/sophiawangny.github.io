R E A L   P A N D E M I C

A podcast produced over quarantine

What:

   Side Project

When:

   July

Where:

   Remote

Tools:

   Voice Compassion Empathy Determination
Countless events and tragedies occurred during the pandemic in this summer of 2020. To discuss the issues that persist in our world today, a few friends and I came up with a podcast called "Coronavirus or Colored Virus: The Real Pandemic", to create a space to educate others, amplify our voices and most importantly, the voices of others. Our goal is to bring attention to the different issues that revolve around the COVID-19 pandemic and our intersections with the current events as women of color. We won a grant from AAPI Women Lead to create this series.

In the session shown above, Janice, Inaara, and I discuss chinatown businesses, the history of the Model Minority myth, debunking it to increase solidarity, and why the Black Lives Matter movement is so important. We talk about why it is so important to amplify black voices and have uncomfortable conversations with families and friends about racial justice in America. Click here to check it out.
